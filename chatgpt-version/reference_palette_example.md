# Colour Palette Reference

A good capsule palette is organised into functional groups, not just a list of nice colours. Below is an example structure (Soft Summer, cool-muted) — use this as a template for any user's palette output.

## The four groups

### 1. Core Neutrals — the backbone
The colours your trousers, coats, and foundational pieces live in. 2-4 colours. These are the neutrals that anchor every outfit.

Example (Soft Summer):
- Cool Charcoal — #3a3f4a
- Dove Grey — #7f8a99
- Cool Navy — #2e3b4e
- Soft Black — #1a1a1f

### 2. Near-Face Accents — what flatters close to your face
The colours your tops, collars, scarves, and anything near your face should prioritise. These are the colours that make your skin look alive.

Example (Soft Summer):
- Dusty Rose — #c99eaa
- Soft Teal — #7a9a95
- Sage — #8a9a7a
- Muted Burgundy — #7a3f4f
- Cool Mauve — #a593a0

### 3. Expression Colours — for prints and loud pieces
The bold end of your palette. These appear in prints, statement dresses, and pieces where you want to make noise.

Example (Soft Summer):
- Plum — #5e3d5e
- Dusty Lavender — #9e8ab0
- Cool Berry — #8f4a5e
- Slate Blue — #5c7b8e

### 4. Uniform-Compatible Colours (if relevant)
If the user has a professional uniform — a doctor's coat, a specific company colour — this group lists what sits well next to it. Derived from the full palette, filtered for harmony with the uniform.

Example (Soft Summer under Mehiläinen green):
- Sage, Soft Teal, Muted Burgundy, Cool Charcoal, Plum — these harmonise without clashing with the warm olive coat.

## Avoid list

Specify 3-5 colour categories the user should avoid — or use only far from the face (shoes, bottoms, bags).

Example (things to avoid for Soft Summer):
- Bright warm yellows
- Warm oranges and corals
- Pure bright white
- Cream / ivory
- Warm browns

## How to present the palette

Always include:
- **Visual swatches** (not just colour names) — either an HTML file, DALL·E image, or clear hex codes the user can look up
- **Short rationale** for each group
- **Cross-check** against any uniform constraint
- **Avoid list** with reason

Do not deliver a palette as just a bullet list of colour names. Names without visuals are useless.

## Palette by season type (quick reference)

| Season | Character | Key direction |
|---|---|---|
| Winter (Clear Deep Cool) | Cool, deep, high-contrast | True black, pure white, jewel tones |
| Summer (Soft Light Cool) | Cool, soft, muted | Dusty pastels, soft greys, muted mauves |
| Autumn (Soft Deep Warm) | Warm, muted, earthy | Olive, rust, mustard, camel, terracotta |
| Spring (Clear Light Warm) | Warm, bright, clear | Peach, coral, warm yellow, fresh green |

This is the simplified 4-season model. Modern analysis often uses 12 or 16 sub-types. Always go with the user's specific analysis result, not a generic season.
